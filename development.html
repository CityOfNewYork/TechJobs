Senior Mobile Application Developer
Information Technology and Telecommunications

Title: Senior Mobile Application Developer

Civil Service Title: Computer Specialist (Software)

Agency: Department of Information Technology and Telecommunications

Salary: $87,203 – 115,000

What the Agency Does: The NYC Department of Information Technology and Telecommunications (DoITT) is responsible for maintaining the foundational IT infrastructure and systems that touch every aspect of City life from public safety to human services, from education to economic development crossing the full spectrum of governmental operations.

What You Will Do: As part of the Mobile Application Development team, you will work with the scrum master, mobile UX designer and the technical lead to design and develop native mobile applications for iOS and Android platforms for NYC. You will build native mobile applications using standard design patterns and best practices for a variety of strategic initiatives, such as NYC 311, that impact NYC residents, businesses and visitors. Your key tasks will include:

> Leading the design and development of native iOS applications in Objective-C and native Android applications in Java.

> Collaborating with business analysts, mobile UX designers, and project managers to determine timeline and detailed tasks.

> Mentoring junior developers in order to create a top-notch application development team.

The Ideal Candidate:
> An all-star developer with deep experience building large scale native mobile applications, as well as integrating mobile applications with backend J2EE/.NET systems via web services.

> A master of mobile development (Objective-C/Swift, HTML5, Javascript, CSS3 and AJAX) and industry standards, best practices and trends.

> A driven problem-solver with a passion for complex government, urban and social challenges.

Minimum Requirements:
BA and two years of relevant work experience.

Program Lead - Interagency Mapping Portal
City Planning

Title: Program Lead - Interagency Mapping Portal

Civil Service Title: Computer Specialist (Software)

Agency: Department of City Planning

Salary: $77,157 – 88,000

What the Agency Does: The Department of City Planning (DCP) is responsible for planning the growth of the City of New York to enhance quality of life for all New Yorkers. DCP promotes coordinated investments in infrastructure and services, provides analysis for the development of neighborhood-based plans, and supports resilient and sustainable communities across the five boroughs for a more equitable New York City.

What the Division Does: The Capital Planning division of City Planning is spearheading innovative approaches to interagency collaboration and strategic capital program development through the use of data and data visualization platforms.

What You Will Do: You will lead the development, implementation, and ongoing advancement of a new mapping portal that can provide a common operational picture for planning across City government. Your key tasks will include:

> Managing the development and interagency adoption of an integrated map-based data visualization and analytics portal that incorporates and aligns City-owned and operated assets, public facilities databases, capital needs assessments, capital investment programs, and other related spatial datasets.

> Working across City agencies, with the Office of Management & Budget, as well as several Mayor’s Offices to advance data sharing standards for capital planning.

> Developing new analytics from the integration of disparate datasets and generating insights to better inform decision-makers, automating these analyses when appropriate.

The Ideal Candidate:
> An all-star developer with deep experience in spatial data, data integration, data visualization, user experience, interaction design, and quantified communities.

> A master of product and program management or technology systems development.

> A driven entrepreneur with a passion for contributing to the professional development of staff.

Minimum Requirements:
BA and two years of relevant work experience.
